## Entry 4: Fluid, Mixture, and Sensing Calculations

**Date:** 2/26/26  

**Objective:** Document the fluid-mixing equations and tolerance analysis used to justify the feasibility of accurate nicotine concentration control.

**Work Completed:**  
The team wrote the fluid, mixture, and sensing subsystem section. The Home Base design used separate nicotine and diluent reservoirs, peristaltic pumps, load cells with HX711 amplifiers, and an optical concentration verification sensor. The design document included the volumetric dilution relationship used to calculate the required liquid volumes for a desired target concentration: `C_stock * V_stock = C_target * V_total`, `V_stock = (C_target * V_total) / C_stock`, and `V_diluent = V_total - V_stock`. For example, if the stock solution is `5.0%` nicotine and the desired daily dose is `10.0 mL` at `3.0%`, then the system requires `6.00 mL` of stock solution and `4.00 mL` of diluent. The tolerance analysis also considered a worst-case pump error where the nicotine pump over-delivers and the diluent pump under-delivers.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The calculations showed that a `±2%` pump error would only create about a `±0.05%` absolute concentration error in the example case. This supported the feasibility of using pump-based mixing, provided the pumps could be calibrated accurately. The team also decided that load cells and optical sensing would be important feedback mechanisms because pump timing alone might not be reliable enough for final verification.

**Next Step:**  
Select specific pumps, load cells, and optical sensing parts, then prepare verification tests for pump accuracy and load-cell resolution.
