## Entry 3: Early Risk Identification

**Date:** 2/6/26  

**Objective:** Finalize the early project approval direction and identify the first technical risks.

**Work Completed:**  
The team reviewed what needed to be included for project approval and discussed the project’s main feasibility risks. These included whether small pumps could accurately dispense liquid, whether the system could safely measure or estimate nicotine concentration, whether wireless communication would be reliable, and whether the design could be demonstrated within the semester timeline. We also discussed user safety constraints, such as limiting dose size, tracking usage, and preventing uncontrolled nicotine delivery.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The team identified fluid delivery accuracy, sensor feedback, and safety logic as core verification areas. We also decided that the project would need a clear separation between the user-facing interface and the safety-critical homebase controller. Early algorithm equations were not finalized yet, but the team agreed that the concentration update should depend on both puff behavior and user condition.

**Next Step:**  
Move into subsystem planning, assign responsibilities, and start building a preliminary BOM and schedule.
