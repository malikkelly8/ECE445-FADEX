## Entry 2: Final Demonstration Execution

**Date:** 4/28/26  

**Objective:** Demonstrate the integrated FadeX system to course staff and show progress toward the major design requirements.

**Work Completed:**  
The team performed the final demonstration using the available hardware and software integration. The demo focused on the homebase system receiving user or puff input, running the adaptive algorithm, calculating a target concentration or dose, and using pumps/load-cell feedback to deliver liquid. The team also showed the user-facing interface through Blynk or a serial-based backup workflow, depending on connection reliability. Safety and verification features were emphasized, including load-cell taring, staged pump control, emergency stop behavior, and limits on uncontrolled dosing.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The final demonstration showed that the project had achieved a working proof of concept for adaptive, feedback-based fluid delivery. Some parts of the original proposal changed due to feasibility, safety, and time constraints, but the core idea remained: use user behavior and software control to guide a tapering process, then physically produce a controlled mixture. The demo also provided important evidence for what worked, what needed more refinement, and what would be described in the final report.
