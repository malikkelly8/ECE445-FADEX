## Entry 2: Design Review Feedback and Technical Risk Refinement

**Date:** 3/3/26  

**Objective:** Use design review feedback to identify the highest-risk parts of the project and refine the implementation plan.

**Work Completed:**  
After design review discussions, the team focused on the subsystems most likely to affect final success: pump accuracy, concentration measurement, load-cell feedback, safety logic, and wireless communication. We discussed that the project could not rely only on theoretical pump timing because tubing, pump startup behavior, and liquid movement could introduce error. We also recognized that measuring the final concentration would be difficult if nicotine could not be used directly in the lab, so the team needed to keep investigating safer surrogate chemicals and optical sensing methods.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The team decided that the project should prioritize proof-of-concept testing for the fluid delivery system and concentration sensing system. The design review reinforced the need for modular verification: first prove that the ESP32 can control pumps, then prove that load cells can measure delivered mass, then integrate those results into the algorithm. This helped reduce risk by making each subsystem testable before full system integration.

**Next Step:**  
Update the design plan and begin preparing breadboard tests for pump control, load-cell measurement, and communication.
