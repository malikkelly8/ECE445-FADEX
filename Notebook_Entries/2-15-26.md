## Entry 2: Nicotine Surrogate and Measurement Problem

**Date:** 2/15/26  

**Objective:** Identify unresolved technical problems that could block the project before detailed hardware design.

**Work Completed:**  
The team identified that one of the largest unresolved issues was how to safely replace nicotine for the course demonstration and how to measure the concentration of that replacement liquid. Since nicotine is toxic and difficult to handle safely, the team needed a surrogate material that could represent nicotine concentration without creating the same health risk. We also discussed that the project needed a sensing method that could verify concentration, not just assume the pumps mixed the correct ratio.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The team recognized that the nicotine replacement and concentration-sensing method would strongly affect the final design. If the team could not safely use nicotine, the system would need to demonstrate the same control concept using a measurable surrogate. This decision later supported the move toward using a vitamin C/ascorbic acid surrogate and a UV-based concentration sensor, but at this stage the problem was still open.

**Next Step:**  
Research safe surrogate chemicals and sensing methods that could verify concentration changes during mixing.
