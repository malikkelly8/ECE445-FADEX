## Entry 4: Verification Planning for Breadboard Demo

**Date:** 3/6/26  

**Objective:** Turn the design review plan into concrete subsystem tests for the upcoming breadboard demo.

**Work Completed:**  
The team began planning verification tests that could be performed before full system integration. Proposed tests included programming the ESP32 and confirming GPIO output, driving pumps through motor drivers, reading load-cell data through HX711 amplifiers, checking ADC input from the photodiode circuit, generating PWM for the UV LED, and sending simulated puff data through the software. The team also discussed how the adaptive algorithm could be tested in simulation before being connected to real hardware.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The team decided that the breadboard demo should focus on showing measurable progress rather than a fully integrated final product. The most important demo goals were proving that the ESP32 could control hardware, that pumps could move liquid, that sensors could produce readable values, and that the algorithm could calculate a next concentration from user or puff inputs. This created a clearer testing path for the next week.

**Next Step:**  
Build the first breadboard tests, write simple ESP32 bring-up code, and prepare evidence such as serial monitor outputs, oscilloscope waveforms, and photos of the test setup.
