## Entry 2: Meeting With Shrey and Detector Housing Discussion

**Date:** 3/9/26  
**Objective:** Discuss concentration sensing, detector housing needs, pump options, and software direction with Shrey.

**Work Completed:**  
The team met with Shrey and discussed several important design issues. For the detector system, we asked about creating a housing and identified the need to send specifications for the optical components along with a sketch of the proposed layout. The team also discussed chemical ordering and the need to coordinate with Elbashir. Pump selection was another major topic, especially whether Bartel pumps would meet our accuracy needs and arrive in time. The team also discussed software topics such as using a medical record number instead of personally identifiable information, estimating the maximum liquid volume needed for a taper schedule, and using usage data to adjust tapering over time.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The meeting helped clarify that concentration measurement and pump selection were still high-risk areas. The team decided to keep investigating detector housing, pump availability, chemical ordering, and tapering strategy. The discussion also reinforced the idea that the algorithm should not only follow a fixed schedule but should collect usage data and adjust the taper based on behavior.

**Next Step:**  
Send detector part specifications and layout sketches for housing support, contact pump vendors if needed, and continue refining the tapering algorithm.
