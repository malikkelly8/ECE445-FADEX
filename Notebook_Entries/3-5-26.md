## Entry 3: PCB Planning and Parts Ordering

**Date:** 3/5/26  

**Objective:** Prepare for the second-round PCB order deadline and identify which circuits needed to be finalized or revised.

**Work Completed:**  
The team reviewed the circuits that needed to be included on the custom PCBs, including the ESP32 controller, power regulation, pump driver connections, load-cell amplifier connections, photodiode/UV sensing circuitry, and handheld vape control circuitry. We also compared this PCB plan against the parts list from the design document to make sure the required components were accounted for. Because several parts had long lead times, the team discussed ordering parts quickly while keeping a breadboard fallback plan available.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The team decided that the PCB design should remain modular so individual circuits could be tested separately. This was important because the final system included several uncertain subsystems, and a single PCB issue could delay the entire project. The team also kept the option of breadboarding the Home Base control system if the PCB arrived late or required revision.

**Next Step:**  
Submit or prepare the PCB order, continue component sourcing, and begin writing skeleton code for hardware bring-up tests.
