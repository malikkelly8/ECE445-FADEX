## Entry 3: Pump and Chemical Strategy

**Date:** 3/11/26  

**Objective:** Evaluate pump options and identify a safe chemical strategy for demonstrating concentration measurement.

**Work Completed:**  
The team continued discussing pump options and whether higher-cost precision pumps were justified for the project. Since accurate dosing was one of the most important requirements, the team considered whether spending more on pumps or related driver hardware could reduce risk. We also continued discussing the challenge of replacing nicotine for the demonstration. The team needed a chemical surrogate that would be safer to handle than nicotine but still measurable by the selected sensing method.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The team recognized that pump accuracy and chemical sensing were linked: even if the pumps delivered the correct volume, the system still needed a way to verify concentration. This supported the continued investigation of a UV-based sensing approach and a safer surrogate material. The team also recognized that final pump selection had to balance cost, lead time, electrical complexity, and accuracy.

**Next Step:**  
Continue sourcing pumps, verify lead times, and research surrogate chemicals that can be detected with optical sensing.
