## Entry 4: Parts Procurement and Nicotine Contingency Planning

**Date:** 4/16/26  

**Objective:** Review remaining parts procurement issues and develop a backup plan if nicotine could not be used safely in the lab.

**Work Completed:**  
The team discussed uncertainty around using real nicotine and whether safer substitute materials would be needed for the final demo. We also reviewed component sourcing, including possible substitutions for MOSFETs, LED drivers, USB-C parts, and other hardware. Since some parts were coming from different vendors, including DigiKey and Amazon, the team discussed how to keep progress moving even if the final PCB or specialty components were delayed.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The team recognized that a nicotine surrogate might be necessary for safety and feasibility. This decision later supported the use of vitamin C/ascorbic acid as a measurable surrogate for concentration testing. The team also decided to prioritize parts that enabled subsystem testing, even if the exact final hardware was not fully available yet.

**Next Step:**  
Finalize the parts list, continue ordering needed components, and confirm with TAs or course staff what materials could be used safely for demonstration.
