# Week of 2/9/26

## Entry 1: Proposal Planning and High-Level Requirements

**Date:** 2/9/26  

**Objective:** Convert the FadeX concept into a formal project proposal with a clear problem statement, solution, and high-level requirements.

**Work Completed:**  
The team began organizing the project proposal around the public health problem of nicotine addiction and the limitations of current cessation tools. We identified that gum and patches do not address the behavioral habit of inhalation/oral fixation, while manual tapering is difficult because users must buy specific nicotine concentrations and often face large concentration jumps. The proposed FadeX solution was written as a Bluetooth-enabled vaporization device with two reservoirs: one for high-concentration nicotine and one for zero-nicotine diluent. The system would use pumps controlled by an ESP32 to mix liquids according to a tapering schedule set through a mobile application.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The team defined three initial high-level requirements for the proposal: the system should support wireless control through a preset tapering algorithm, the system should mix nicotine and diluent with less than 20% error, and the firmware should enforce a maximum nicotine delivery cap based on puff duration and current concentration. These requirements gave the project measurable goals and connected the user-facing app, embedded software, and fluid delivery hardware into one system.
