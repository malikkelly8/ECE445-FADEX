## Entry 3: Tolerance Analysis and Fluid Delivery Risk

**Date:** 2/11/26 

**Objective:** Analyze the main engineering risks related to liquid delivery accuracy and nicotine concentration control.

**Work Completed:**  
The team wrote the tolerance analysis section of the proposal, focusing on the difficulty of accurately delivering a commanded nicotine concentration inside a small vaping device. We identified several fluid-control risks, including unwanted backflow, pressure from user inhalation causing unintended flow, pump variability, and contamination between nicotine and diluent paths. The proposal explained why micro-peristaltic pumps were attractive for this design: when stopped, the compressed tubing acts similarly to a normally closed check valve, which helps reduce backflow and uncontrolled liquid motion.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The proposal allowed an initial concentration error target of less than 20%, with the expectation that the final system should aim to perform better. This tolerance was chosen because the project was still in an early feasibility stage, and the team expected pump calibration, tubing behavior, and sensing accuracy to become major debugging areas later. The team also recognized that fluid delivery accuracy would likely become one of the most important verification tests for the project.

**Next Step:**  
Research pump options, valve needs, and sensing methods that could improve mixture accuracy and make the fluid subsystem easier to verify.
