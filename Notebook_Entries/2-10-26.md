## Entry 2: System Block Diagram and Subsystem Breakdown

**Date:** 2/10/26  

**Objective:** Define the main FadeX subsystems and describe how they interact in the full device design.

**Work Completed:**  
The team developed the first formal subsystem breakdown for the proposal. The proposed design included five major subsystems: power and energy management, fluid/mixture/sensing, thermal and aerosol generation, control/display/connectivity, and the mobile application system. The power subsystem was based on a single-cell 18650 Li-ion battery with charging and regulation circuitry. The fluid subsystem used two micro-peristaltic pumps to meter nicotine and diluent into a mixing chamber. The thermal subsystem used a resistive heating coil controlled by a MOSFET, while the control subsystem used an ESP32 to execute the tapering algorithm, enforce safety limits, log usage, and communicate with the mobile app.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The team selected the ESP32 as the main control platform because it supported embedded control, wireless communication, and sensor interfacing. The dual-reservoir architecture was chosen for the proposal because it allowed the nicotine concentration to be reduced continuously rather than in large manual steps. The team also decided that the app should provide schedule control and analytics, while firmware would remain responsible for safety-critical functions such as pump control, puff tracking, and lockouts.
