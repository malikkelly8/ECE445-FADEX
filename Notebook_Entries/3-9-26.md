## Entry 1: Breadboard Demo Planning

**Date:** 3/9/26  

**Objective:** Prepare for the breadboard demo by identifying which hardware and software functions could be demonstrated before full PCB integration.

**Work Completed:**  
The team reviewed the upcoming breadboard demo expectations and focused on creating tests that could prove early subsystem functionality. We discussed which parts of the system were ready to test, including ESP32 programming, pump driver outputs, load-cell reading, and early software logic. The team also discussed how to present the project even if all final parts were not available yet. The breadboard plan focused on demonstrating progress toward the Home Base mixing system rather than trying to show the full final vape design.

**Results / Decisions:**  
The team decided that the breadboard demo should emphasize modular subsystem tests. The key goal was to show that the ESP32 could control outputs, communicate with sensors, and eventually coordinate the pumps. This approach made the demo more realistic because the PCB and final mechanical system were still under development.

**Next Step:**  
Prepare breadboard wiring, write simple ESP32 test sketches, and collect evidence of working hardware tests.
